Involving Youth in Social Issues

Together with the US Embassy NBF has worked on promoting some burning social issues in addition to it signature "Say No to Drugs and Violence!". These include environment and gender based violence.

NBF has gained considerable experience in working with these issues through the Youth Leadership Program sponsored by the State Department and organized by Magee Women care International.

Coordination and Network

NBF has established extensive network with like minded organisations working with youth and children and environment too. On homeground, NBF has a loose network and access to over 2000 youth and children in 15 districts of Nepal per courtesy the annual Regional B Boying/B Girling tours sponsored by the US Embassy.

NBF has good relationship with the US Embassy, Rotary (Nepal), KHORYUG (Environment) Nepal network, Godavari Alumni Association (GAA), Himalayan Roots to Fruits and over 50 schools and colleges from which candidates were invited for application for the Youth Leadership Program.

NBF PARTNERSHIP ACTIVITIES WITH THE US EMBASSY, Kathmandu NEPAL

The partnership with the US Embassy, Nepal started in 2010 when the US Embassy co-organized the 2010 National B Boy/B Girl Championship with NBF, commemorating Library Week and promoting Say No to Drugs and Violence. In 2011,2012,2013 and 2014 together with the US Embassy, NBF organized the Regional B Boy/B Girl Selections, promoting of “WE SAY NO TO DRUGS AND VIOLENCE” in the 4 American corners (Biratnagar, Birgunj, Bhairawa,Pokhara,surket,Ilam,Tehrahum,Butwal,Dhangadi) in Nepal. By popular demand Dharan and Chitwan were added on.

Local Rotary clubs were also mobilized to support the events. The US Embassy has also been supporting financial support for the Himalayan International B Boying/B Girling Jatra (a yearly mega international) which has been ongoing every year . This was organized on 1st and 3rd December year ever to commemorate International HIV and AIDS Day with the key message of SAY NO TO DRUGS, HIV AND AIDS.

South Asia Youth Summit 2011, 2012, 2013 and 2014

NBF has been the local partner organization to Magee Woman care International (MWI) for implementing the US State Department’s South Asia Youth Summit from 2011-2014. This programe organizes student exchange with focus on youth leadership training. Each year 6-7 students and one adult facilitator from Nepal are hosted in the US.

NBF is responsible for selection and orientation of Nepal team. NBF also supports, monitors and manages the funds for Community Action Plan project implementation that the SAYS participants are expected to carry out as a part of the SAYS leadership
